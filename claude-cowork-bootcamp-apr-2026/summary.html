Paradigm Shifts

  • From chatbot to operator: Stop thinking of Claude as a chatbot that just generates text. Claude Cowork is an operator and director that performs and executes tasks for you — including actions outside the chat interface like publishing content, scheduling emails, and building pages.
  • From "chat-locked" to end-to-end execution: The old way: prompt AI → get output → manually copy-paste, switch tabs, edit, upload everywhere. The new way with Claude Cowork: prompt or schedule → Claude generates → you review → Claude publishes and executes. The most time-consuming implementation steps are handled for you.
  • From time-saving to exponential returns: Don't see AI as something that saves you time. See it as an amplifier that increases your productivity and throughput 10–100x. Prioritize automations that give you the biggest returns in the shortest time, not just the easiest things to automate.
  • From hiring to delegating to AI: As business owners, you are now "gently forced to delegate" to Claude. The mental shift is moving from "I need to hire someone for this" to "I can instruct Claude to do this" — whether that's creating slides, publishing content, or managing email campaigns.

Golden Nuggets

  • Prioritize for exponential returns, not time savings: When deciding what to automate, ask: "Where will I get the biggest exponential returns in the shortest amount of time with the least energy and investment?" Automating customer support might save time, but automating content creation compounds — more reach, more traffic, more leads, stacking up over time.
  • The $300K wake-up call: Darius was spending $25–30K/month ($300K+ annually) on a marketing team: senior marketer, Instagram agency ($7K/month alone), copywriters, junior designers, and ad teams. A Facebook page ban forced a rethink, and that entire function is now being rebuilt on Claude for ~$10K/month.
  • AI-native, not AI-added: Klemen's first recommendation was to make the entire company AI-native — not just bolt AI onto existing processes, but fundamentally rebuild how marketing operates with Claude at the core.
  • Claude built these very slides: The webclass presentation itself was built entirely by Claude — pure HTML/CSS with 50+ slide components, hosted on GitHub Pages. No Canva, no Google Slides, no PowerPoint. Even last-minute changes 15 minutes before going live were made by instructing Claude in chat.
  • Proactive AI input: Claude can be scheduled to autonomously do research every day and generate fresh content ideas, storing them in Notion — freeing your cognitive power before you even open the laptop.
  • This applies to solopreneurs too: Even if you have a team of one, you still need to perform all marketing functions. Claude becomes the team you don't have, regardless of your business size.
  • Notion is the ultimate collaboration layer: Notion is "hands down the best platform" to store structured and unstructured content that both human beings and AI agents can easily access, edit, and understand. Not Google Docs — Notion.
  • Action videos outperform talking head videos: Simple 5–10 second video clips with text overlays (not talking head) consistently get more engagement. One such reel posted less than 24 hours before the webclass generated 150+ webinar registrations.
  • HTML-first approach for everything: The best way to generate carousels, slides, and pages is to first generate HTML files, then convert to images (JPEG/PNG) when needed. This is faster, cheaper on tokens, and produces higher quality results than any other method.
  • Reduce the cost of producing value, not just time: Inspired by Elon Musk's obsession with reducing production costs — the first version of anything is always the worst, but each next version is better AND takes less time and energy to create.

Action Steps

  1. Identify your highest-return levers: Look at your business and ask where exponential returns live. For most businesses, this is content (reach & traffic), email (nurturing & revenue), and launches (new offers & funnels).
  2. Get your tech stack ready: You need a paid Claude subscription (Claude Cowork), a free Notion account, and a Google account (for Google Drive). That's the foundation — everything else is optional.
  3. Set up your Content Machine (Part 1 — Image content): Start with the quickest wins: image posts and carousels. Claude generates the copy, captions, and images (as HTML converted to JPEG/PNG), stores everything in a Notion database, and publishes to Instagram and LinkedIn after your review.
  4. Set up your Content Machine (Part 2 — Video content): Move to video shorts (Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, LinkedIn, TikTok). Film a simple 5–10 second action clip, let Claude add text overlays and generate the caption. Claude stores the video in Google Drive for review, then publishes to all platforms.
  5. Set up your Email Machine: Use Claude to generate email sequences (promo emails, value emails, follow-up automations). Claude stores drafts in Notion for review, then creates draft campaigns in Klaviyo (or your email platform). A human always does the final review before scheduling.
  6. Set up your Launch Machine: Use Claude to build complete sales funnels: sales pages, checkout pages with order bumps and one-click upsells, webinar registration pages, and lead capture pages — all generated as HTML code and deployed via GitHub Pages.
  7. Adopt the human-in-the-loop workflow for everything: For every automation, follow: Input (prompt or schedule) → Claude generates → You review & approve in Notion → Claude publishes. Never let AI publish without your review.
  8. Start delegating to Claude daily: Shift your mindset from doing to delegating. Schedule Claude to proactively research ideas, draft content, and prepare assets — so your day starts with output ready for review, not a blank screen.

Questions And Answers

  • "What level of knowledge do I need to succeed?"

The emphasis is on learning by doing — you install and modify existing skills first, then build your own. Darius and Klemen are there to help with troubleshooting on every call.
  • "What tech stack do I need to get started?"
    A paid Claude subscription (Claude Cowork), a free Notion account, and a Google account (for Google Drive). That's the essential foundation. You'll also need the social media accounts you plan to publish to (Instagram, LinkedIn, etc.).
  • "Does this apply to my specific business type?"
    Yes — this works for e-learning, courses, digital products, memberships, coaching, consulting, services (remote or local), and even e-commerce. Whether you're a hairstylist, hotel owner, drop shipper, or plumber, at minimum 70% of the program is directly usable. The skills are intelligent and adapt to your business model.
  • "Will we learn to build our own skills, or just install yours?"
    Both. You first install and modify the pre-built skills (easiest way to get started), and through that process you learn the meta skill — how to create any workflow for anything. Klemen will also show you how to build skills from simple to advanced.
  • "Can I transfer this knowledge to another domain like coaching?"
    Absolutely. As long as you are in an ethically acceptable and legal industry, the skills and the entire system are adaptable. The program is created by founders for founders — it's about growing your business, not just installing automations.
  • "Are these slides really built with Claude?"
    Yes. The entire presentation is pure HTML/CSS — a website, essentially — built entirely inside Claude Cowork with 50+ different section components. No Canva, no Google Slides, no PowerPoint. Pushed to GitHub Pages for hosting.
  • "How much time per week should I dedicate?"
    Minimum 2–3 hours per week. 60–90 minutes for the live class, plus implementation time. The more you put in, the faster you see results. The emphasis is on doing, not just watching.
  • "Will workshops be recorded?"
    Yes. All sessions are recorded and available as modules you can watch at your own pace. Nothing is held back.
  • "Can my team member join the calls?"
    Yes — you can bring your marketing manager or a team member to relevant sessions.
  • "Will we get Notion templates too?"
    Yes. Pre-built Notion templates are included that you can clone or import into your own account. The skills themselves will also propose an initial structure even without the templates.
  • "Can I supply my own photos and videos?"
    Absolutely. Save your images or videos in a folder, point Claude to the location, and it will use your own media in the content it generates.
  • "What about blogging?"
    Klemen's advice: start with publishing text content on external platforms like LinkedIn first, where you rank faster in Google and AI search, rather than only on your own website.
  • "Do I need both paid ads and organic content?"
    Ideally yes. Start with organic content to learn the ropes, then add paid ads as rocket fuel. You need organic presence because when people see your ads, they will look you up — supporting organic content helps paid ads perform with better ROI. Plus, being over-dependent on paid ads is risky (as Darius learned with the Facebook ban).
